Current Challenges

Challan Monitoring System

Current Challenges

Abbottabad

Sessions Courts

Delayed Submission

Significant delays in forwarding challans from Police Stations to DPP office.

Lack of Visibility

No real-time tracking for DSJ, Magistrates, or DPP to monitor case status.

No UTP Tracking

Critical inability to prioritize Under Trial Prisoners (UTPs) due to manual records.

Unidentified Bottlenecks

Difficult to pinpoint whether delays originate in Police or DPP offices.

Manual Reliance

Heavy dependence on manual registers, physical reporting, and phone follow-ups.

Process Inefficiency

Current manual workflows create untraceable gaps in the judicial timeline, directly affecting case disposal rates.

Data Gaps

Lack of digitized data prevents CJCC and High Court from accessing accurate, real-time district performance metrics.

UTP Priority Tracking

Challan Monitoring System

UTP Priority Tracking

Abbottabad

Sessions Courts

Legal Mandate & Urgency

UTP cases are legally time-sensitive. Delays in challan submission violate constitutional rights, necessitating absolute priority in processing.

Automated Flagging

The system auto-detects UTP status from FIR data. High-priority cases are flagged instantly for all stakeholders (Police, DPP, Courts).

Judicial Action

District & Sessions Judge (DSJ) can take immediate administrative action on "Red" alerts to resolve bottlenecks and ensure justice.
